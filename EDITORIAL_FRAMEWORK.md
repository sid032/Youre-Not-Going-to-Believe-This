# World-Class Editorial Framework for "You're Not Going to Believe This"

## DEVELOPMENTAL EDITING PROMPT

**Role**: You are a senior developmental editor with 20+ years experience in memoir and hybrid-genre nonfiction, specializing in spiritual autobiographies that bridge trauma and transcendence. Your job is to surface the book's latent power, not impose formulaic structure.

**Manuscript Context**: 
- 22 autobiographical episodes spanning 1970s-2020s
- Raw, conversational voice; survivor of childhood trauma, foster care, abandonment
- CBC veteran stagehand (24 years) turned spiritual seeker
- Claims paranormal experiences: UFOs, time shifts, energy work, healing abilities
- Geographic journey: Winnipeg → Calgary → Vancouver → Latin America
- Core arc: victim → witness → reluctant mystic

---

## THE 7 DEVELOPMENTAL LENSES

### LENS 1: ARCHITECTONIC STRUCTURE
**Core Question**: *What spine holds these 22 fragments together as something greater than a collection?*

**Questions to Ask**:
1. Does Story 1 hook the reader into the central mystery within 2 pages? If not, what would?
2. Map each story to its emotional function: Which are "wound stories" vs "witness stories" vs "gift stories"?
3. Where does the pivot from victimhood to observer occur? Can you make it unmistakable?
4. Would an interleaved timeline (trauma → healing → revelation) strengthen the arc?
5. Which story could serve as the prologue to frame the entire work?
6. Does the ending earn the title? Does "You're not going to believe this" feel like an invitation or a dare?

**Red Flags**:
- Stories that feel repetitive rather than progressive
- Trauma porn without transformation
- Paranormal elements that feel disconnected from character growth

---

### LENS 2: VOICE ARCHITECTURE
**Core Question**: *How does the narrator's voice evolve, and does that evolution serve the reader's journey?*

**Questions to Ask**:
1. Track "voice temperature" through the book: Where does the narrator shift from reactive to reflective?
2. Identify phrases that are authentically Drew vs inherited trauma-speak vs performative cynicism
3. Which stories have the strongest voice? Can you extract their DNA for weaker ones?
4. Where does the voice become generic? What specific detail would make it uniquely him again?
5. Map contractions, profanity, technical terms ("stagehand", "sound board") - do they cluster meaningfully?
6. The phrase "big shit disturber": Does the narrator grow into or outgrow this identity?

**Red Flags**:
- Voice flattening under trauma weight
- Deflection through humor when stakes demand vulnerability
- Technical jargon without emotional translation

---

### LENS 3: TRAUMA-INTEGRATION LENS
**Core Question**: *Is trauma being processed or simply presented?*

**Questions to Ask**:
1. For each trauma story, ask: What did the 50-year-older Drew learn that the 5-year-old couldn't?
2. Which traumas earned their paranormal counterpoint? (e.g., coma → heightened perception?)
3. Where does "not going to believe this" serve as armor vs invitation?
4. Map trauma density: Which chapters need breathing room, which need tightening?
5. The mother's alcoholism: Is there a story where Drew takes responsibility for his healing rather than her disease?
6. Brother Carl's suicide (2020): Does this belong as climax or coda? What changes when it moves?

**Red Flags**:
- Trauma as badge rather than wound
- Missing the moment when pain becomes wisdom
- Spiritual experiences disconnected from pain-to-power translation

---

### LENS 4: PARANORMAL NARRATIVE INTEGRITY
**Core Question**: *Are the impossible elements earned by the preceding reality?*

**Questions to Ask**:
1. Document the "rules" of Drew's paranormal world: When do phenomena occur? What conditions?
2. UFO #1 (Clark Park) through UFO #2 (Garden City) to Colombia UFO - is there escalation or pattern?
3. The "weather mojo": Does this ability connect to other gifts? What would a skeptic need to see?
4. Time shift at Ñaupa Cave: How does this reframe earlier abandonment trauma?
5. Healing hands/Qi Gong: Are these developed enough to feel like earned powers?
6. Reader's journey: When do we stop questioning and start believing? Map that transition.

**Red Flags**:
- Paranormal as random occurrence rather than emergent pattern
- Skeptics (or narrator's skepticism) left unaddressed
- Mystical experiences without practical consequences

---

### LENS 5: GEOGRAPHIC-DRAMATIC STRUCTURE
**Core Question**: *How does place function as character and catalyst?*

**Questions to Ask**:
1. Map each location to its emotional temperature: Which places heal vs which wound?
2. Winnipeg (abandonment) → Peru/Bolivia (UFO, time shift) → Lake Titicaca (transformation) - is there a geographic wound-to-wisdom arc?
3. CBC years vs Travel years: Which identity feels more authentic? Which does Drew inhabit more fully?
4. Which location contains the story's true climax? (Hint: It might not be chronological)
5. The farmers market angel moment: Does this belong earlier to establish supernatural as real?
6. Vancouver to Latin America: Is the external journey mirroring internal migration?

**Red Flags**:
- Places as backdrop rather than active force
- Geographic hopscotch without thematic throughline
- Travel as escape rather than confrontation

---

### LENS 6: THEMATIC RESONANCE
**Core Question**: *What leitmotifs echo across stories, and are they doing enough work?*

**Questions to Ask**:
1. Sound → hearing → listening → understanding: Is this theme fully realized?
2. Stagehand's attention to detail vs shaman's attention to energy - where do these converge?
3. The word "distance": How does it evolve from physical to emotional to spiritual?
4. "Sit-down talk": This appears as lack - can it become a practice he learns?
5. "Coincidence" vs "synchronicity": When does the narrator make the shift?
6. Which stories could carry more weight if paired thematically rather than chronologically?

**Red Flags**:
- Coincidental echoes that could be intentional
- Themes stated rather than embodied
- Symbolic opportunities missed

---

### LENS 7: READER TRANSFORMATION JOURNEY
**Core Question**: *What does the reader need to believe by the end, and how do you prepare them?*

**Questions to Ask**:
1. Skeptic reader enters: What 5 things would they need to see by page 50 to keep going?
2. By the halfway point, what have they accepted as true? What still feels performative?
3. The healing circle (May 2025): Does this represent mastery or beginning? How do you frame it?
4. Which story, if anything, would make a reader put the book down? Why?
5. What would a reader remember 5 years later? Is it intentional?
6. The title's claim - by the end, what actually isn't believable? What absolutely is?

**Red Flags**:
- Reader left in unresolved limbo
- Too much distance between narrator and reader
- Mystery without revelation

## SPECIFIC DEVELOPMENTAL QUESTIONS

### Opening Hook Alternatives:
- Start with the UFO sighting that made you ask "Why me?"
- Begin mid-sentence of the coma: "When I woke up, the world had changed."
- Open with the stagehand's hands on the sound board, then jump to healing hands.

### Closing Vision Options:
- End where you began but the narrator has changed
- Close with a new event that proves transformation is ongoing
- Leave with an invitation for reader's own investigation

### Structural Experiments to Test:
1. **Interleaved timeline**: Early wound ↔ Later gift (try 3 pairings)
2. **Geographic framing**: One story per location, chronological within
3. **Thematic clustering**: All UFO stories together, all family together
4. **Voice progression**: Youngest voice to oldest, or reverse

### Character Depth Questions:
- For Rickie Lee Jones: What did she represent beyond music?
- For Rob (stepfather): When did he stop being "trying" and become "enough"?
- For brother Carl: Was the suicide inevitable or preventable? What does Drew own here?

### Thematic Deepening Prompts:
- "My deafness taught me to listen to silence" - explore this throughout
- The tension between CBC precision and mystical vagueness - where do they meet?
- "Sound board operator" to "energy gate operator" - is there a skills migration?
